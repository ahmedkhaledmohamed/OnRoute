# Ideas Backlog

Living list of feature ideas and product directions. When the time comes, we pick one, think deeper, plan, then execute.

---

## Multi-Stop Routes
Multiple errands in one trip. Enter A → B and add 3 things you need (coffee, pharmacy, groceries). OnRoute finds the optimal order and locations that minimize total detour. Not just one stop — a full errand run optimized for time.

## Complimentary to Google Maps
Don't replace Google Maps — enhance it. OnRoute finds the stop, Google Maps navigates to it. Deepen the handoff: pre-populate the waypoint, sync the travel mode, maybe even a widget or share extension that lets you search from inside Google Maps.

## Beauty & Addictive Behavior
Make the app feel delightful, not just functional. Smooth animations on route rendering. Satisfying haptics when a great stop is found. A "detour score" that gamifies efficient stops. Streaks for daily commuters ("5-day streak of <3 min detours"). The kind of polish that makes people show it to friends.

## Social Loop
See what stops your friends make on their commutes. "Ahmed grabbed coffee at Pilot adding only 2 min." Share your best finds. A lightweight social layer — not a feed, just a "friends' recent stops" card. Creates word-of-mouth and FOMO.

## Kill Time Between Appointments
"I have 2 hours between my doctor appointment and the gym." OnRoute shows what's reachable in that window — cafes to work from, parks to walk, restaurants for a quick lunch. Not route-based but time-budget-based. Input: current location + time available. Output: ranked options by type and distance.

## Tasklist On The Way
"On my way home I need to: pick up prescription, grab groceries, get dinner. Must be done by 6pm." OnRoute takes a tasklist with categories + a deadline, then finds the optimal route that hits all of them within the time constraint. Pharmacy → grocery store → restaurant, ordered to minimize total time.

## Saved Routes & Routines
"Every morning commute, show me coffee." Save your daily route and get one-tap search. Over time, learn your preferences — you always pick indie coffee shops over chains, you never stop at places rated below 4.2. Corridor memory: "3 new places opened on your commute this month."

## Offline / Low Connectivity Mode
Cache the last few route corridors and their POIs. If you lose signal (tunnel, rural area), still show cached results. Useful for road trips through areas with spotty coverage.
